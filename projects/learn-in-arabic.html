a studio built from scratch.

An educational YouTube channel needed a full visual identity and a real shooting environment — from logo and thumbnails to DIY studio sets, lighting, and on-screen graphics.

01 / The Brief

No studio, no system, tight budget.

MSP Tech Club wanted to launch Learn in Arabic — Arabic-first educational videos covering tech and design fundamentals. The challenge was twofold: there was no physical recording space, and no on-screen visual system. Both had to be built before a single video could ship.

📸 Studio set hero · 21:9
02 / The Approach

Build the room, then the brand.

I designed and built the studio environment first — backdrops, three-point lighting setups, sound treatment — using accessible materials. With the room dialed in, I then designed the on-screen system: channel logo, intro/outro motion, thumbnail grid system, and lower-thirds.

What was shipped:

  • DIY studio set + lighting + audio treatment
  • Channel logo + variants for thumbnails and social
  • Thumbnail template grid for visual consistency
  • Motion intro/outro + lower-third graphics package
  • Recognized as Best Leader of the Season — Illustrations Team (2019–2020)

03 / Outcome

A repeatable production system.

The studio kept producing for two seasons under the system I built. The thumbnail and motion templates meant each new video could be produced quickly without sacrificing brand consistency — exactly the multiplier effect a young channel needs.
