01 / The Brief

A company with no visual voice.

When I joined Nile Pharma Food in late 2020, the company had a product line growing fast but no cohesive visual identity to support it. There was a logo, but no system around it — no palette discipline, no typography hierarchy, no templates. Each piece of communication looked like it came from a different company.

The brief was clear in scope but vast in detail: build a visual system that would scale across packaging, social, retail, B2B sales decks, and a corporate website — and make it consistent, recognisable, and trusted in the pharma space.

02 / The Approach

Strategy first. System next.

Before drawing a shape, I ran a full brand audit and competitor analysis across the local and regional pharma space. The category was crowded, but visually conservative — every brand looked like every other brand. The opportunity was to be both professionally credible and visually distinct.

Key decisions:

  • Built a positioning around clarity and trust — not "innovation" buzzwords the category was tired of
  • Designed a logo system with a primary, horizontal lockup, and submark — built for everything from a vitamin bottle to a billboard
  • Developed a strict typography hierarchy and color palette with WCAG-compliant contrast ratios
  • Created a complete template library: social (FB, IG, LinkedIn), sales decks, product sheets, retail signage
  • Designed and launched nilepharmafood.com on WordPress with full mobile responsiveness

03 / Outcome

A brand that compounds.

Six months after the rebrand rolled out, the numbers told the story: customer engagement on social up 25%, online visibility up 40%, and — most importantly — internal teams (sales, accounting, warehouse) stopped second-guessing what was "on-brand" and what wasn't. The system did its job.
